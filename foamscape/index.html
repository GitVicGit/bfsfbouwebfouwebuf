Foamscape

The installation centres on a suspended, partially burnt driftwood trunk held in a state that feels both precarious and unresolved. Accompanied by an audio work assembled from scientific research and news reports on wildfires across the world, the piece brings together sculpture, sound, scent and space to reflect on ecological instability, endurance and the ongoing nature of damage.

Loud Speaker considers how material can hold different forms of stress and transformation at once. The work moves between support and collapse, silence and transmission, landscape and architecture. The sculpture’s presence is extended by the smell of pine tar.
